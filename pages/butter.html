Homemade Butter

Description

Creating your own homemade butter is a delightful culinary adventure that connects you with the heart of traditional cooking. With just one ingredient – heavy cream – and a bit of patience, you can transform a humble dairy product into a creamy, velvety spread that elevates every meal it graces.

Begin by pouring the heavy cream into a food processor or stand mixer and blend it until it thickens, eventually reaching the stage where the butterfat separates from the liquid, leaving you with rich, golden butter. This process not only gives you a deeper appreciation for the food you consume but also allows you to customize your butter with herbs, spices, or even a touch of honey, making it uniquely yours. The satisfaction that comes from spreading your homemade creation on warm, fresh bread, or using it to enhance the flavors of your favorite dishes, is unparalleled. Homemade butter is more than just a condiment; it's a labor of love that turns a simple meal into a culinary masterpiece.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 5 tablespoons ice water

Steps

  1. Pour the cold cream into your food processor. You can also use an electric whisk or stand mixer.
  2. Whip the cream until it separates. After around 2 ½ minutes you will see it start to over whip and really thicken. Keep going. After around 4 minutes it should be fully separated. You can see yellow buttery solids and cloudy liquid at this point.
  3. Once it separates pour in your ice-cold water. This helps separate the butter fully separate from the liquid (buttermilk) and you will see even more buttermilk appear.
  4. Place a sieve over a bowl and pour in the contents of the bowl.
  5. The remaining liquid is buttermilk, you can save this for baking or for meat marinade
  6. Squeeze the butter solids with your hands to make sure no buttermilk remains. You can also use a clean tea towl or cheese cloth.
  7. Stir in some salt, this recipe creates 300g of butter
  8. Can be stored for up to 6 weeks in the fridge.
